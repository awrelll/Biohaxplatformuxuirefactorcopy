## Premium Diagnostic Tests

### üß¨ TrueAge Epigenetic Testing

**Measure biological age vs chronological age through DNA methylation**

#### What is Epigenetic Age Testing?
- Analyzes chemical modifications to DNA (methylation patterns)
- More accurate than chronological age for health outcomes
- Tracks aging at cellular level
- Reversible through lifestyle interventions

#### Key Metrics

##### 1. DunedinPACE (Pace of Aging)
- **What it measures:** Rate of biological aging per calendar year
- **Optimal:** <1.0 (aging slower than chronological time)
- **Average:** 1.0 (aging at normal rate)
- **Concerning:** >1.2 (aging faster than normal)
- **Use case:** Track aging acceleration/deceleration over time

##### 2. Biological Age
- **What it measures:** Overall cellular age
- **Goal:** Be younger biologically than chronologically
- **Example:** 35-year-old with biological age of 28

##### 3. Organ-Specific Aging
- **Brain age** - Cognitive decline risk
- **Heart age** - Cardiovascular risk
- **Liver age** - Metabolic health
- **Immune age** - Immunosenescence
- **Hormone age** - Endocrine function

##### 4. Telomere Length
- **What it measures:** Protective caps on chromosomes
- **Significance:** Shorter telomeres = increased aging
- **Modifiable:** Exercise, stress management, nutrition can lengthen

##### 5. Immune Cell Composition
- T-cell percentages
- B-cell percentages
- Natural killer cells
- Granulocytes
- **Use:** Detect immunosenescence

#### BioHax Integration

**Workflow:**
1. Order TrueAge test through TruDiagnostic
2. Complete at-home blood spot collection
3. Results emailed as PDF (3-4 weeks)
4. Upload PDF to BioHax
5. Dual-engine AI extracts and interprets data
6. Protocols auto-generated to improve biological age
7. Retest every 6-12 months to track progress

**AI-Generated Protocols Based on Results:**
- High biological age ‚Üí Anti-aging supplement stack
- Rapid Pace of Aging ‚Üí Lifestyle intervention protocol
- Poor brain age ‚Üí Nootropics and cognitive training
- Short telomeres ‚Üí Telomerase activators + stress reduction

**Longitudinal Tracking:**
- Chart biological age over time
- Compare Pace of Aging between tests
- Correlate with biomarker changes
- Visualize organ-specific improvements

### ü¶† Geneva GI Effects Test

**Comprehensive gut health diagnostics for microbiome optimization**

#### What Does It Test?
Advanced stool analysis using:
- **PCR (DNA-based detection)** - Identifies microbes by genetic signature
- **Culture** - Grows bacteria to measure quantities
- **Microscopy** - Visually identifies parasites and yeast

#### Five Pillars of Gut Health

##### 1. Maldigestion
**Tests digestive capacity and nutrient absorption**

Biomarkers:
- **Elastase** - Pancreatic enzyme production
- **Fat absorption** - Steatocrit (fat in stool)
- **Protein digestion** - Undigested muscle fibers
- **Carbohydrate digestion** - pH and fermentation markers

Implications:
- Low elastase ‚Üí Pancreatic insufficiency ‚Üí enzyme supplementation
- High fat in stool ‚Üí Bile acid deficiency ‚Üí ox bile support
- Undigested protein ‚Üí Low stomach acid ‚Üí HCl supplementation

##### 2. Inflammation
**Measures intestinal immune activation**

Biomarkers:
- **Calprotectin** - Neutrophil activation (IBD marker)
- **Lactoferrin** - Intestinal inflammation
- **Secretory IgA** - Mucosal immunity
- **Eosinophil Protein X** - Allergic inflammation

Implications:
- High calprotectin ‚Üí Inflammatory bowel disease ‚Üí anti-inflammatory protocol
- Low sIgA ‚Üí Weak gut barrier ‚Üí immunoglobulins + glutamine
- High EPX ‚Üí Food sensitivities ‚Üí elimination diet

##### 3. Dysbiosis
**Analyzes microbial balance and diversity**

Measured:
- **Beneficial bacteria:**
  - Lactobacillus spp.
  - Bifidobacterium spp.
  - Akkermansia muciniphila
  - Faecalibacterium prausnitzii
  
- **Pathogenic bacteria:**
  - C. difficile
  - E. coli (pathogenic strains)
  - Klebsiella
  - Pseudomonas
  
- **Commensal bacteria:**
  - Bacteroides
  - Firmicutes
  - Diversity index

Implications:
- Low beneficial ‚Üí Probiotic supplementation
- High pathogens ‚Üí Antimicrobial herbs or Rx
- Low diversity ‚Üí Prebiotic fiber protocol

##### 4. Metabolite Imbalance
**Measures microbial byproducts**

Biomarkers:
- **Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs):**
  - Butyrate - Colon cell fuel
  - Acetate - Metabolic health
  - Propionate - Glucose regulation
- **pH** - Fermentation health
- **Beta-glucuronidase** - Estrogen metabolism

Implications:
- Low butyrate ‚Üí Resistant starch supplementation
- Low SCFAs overall ‚Üí Increase fiber intake
- High beta-glucuronidase ‚Üí Estrogen dominance risk ‚Üí calcium-d-glucarate

##### 5. Infections
**Detects parasites, yeast, and H. pylori**

Tests for:
- **Parasites:**
  - Giardia lamblia
  - Entamoeba histolytica
  - Blastocystis hominis
  - Cryptosporidium
  
- **Yeast:**
  - Candida albicans
  - Candida tropicalis
  - Saccharomyces cerevisiae
  
- **H. pylori:**
  - Presence/absence
  - Strain typing
  - Virulence factors (CagA, VacA)

Implications:
- Parasites detected ‚Üí Antiparasitic protocol
- Candida overgrowth ‚Üí Antifungal + low-sugar diet
- H. pylori positive ‚Üí Eradication therapy (natural or Rx)

## Glossary

**Key terms used in BioHax:**

- **Biomarker** - Measurable indicator of health status (e.g., cholesterol, HRV)
- **Protocol** - Structured intervention plan (supplements, diet, exercise)
- **Dual-Engine AI** - Cross-verification using OpenBioLLM + Google Gemini
- **Longevity Score** - Overall health optimization metric (0-100)
- **Biological Age** - Cellular age based on epigenetic markers
- **Chronological Age** - Actual age in years
- **HRV (Heart Rate Variability)** - Variation in time between heartbeats (higher = better)
- **CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitor)** - Real-time blood sugar tracking device
- **Epigenetics** - Changes in gene expression without DNA sequence changes
- **Methylation** - Chemical modification of DNA affecting gene expression
- **Pace of Aging** - Rate of biological aging per calendar year
- **SNP (Single Nucleotide Polymorphism)** - Genetic variant
- **Nutrigenomics** - How genes affect response to nutrients
